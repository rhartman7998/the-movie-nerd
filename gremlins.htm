Gremlins: A Holiday Cult Classic with a Mischevious Twist


Gremlins released in 1984 is a cult classic that masterfully blends horror, comedy and holiday cheer. Directed by Joe Dante and written by Chris Columbus. The film in its essence is a cautionary tale wrapped in Christmas lights about what happens when curiosity takes over and the chaos that ensues when rules are not followed.

The movie follows a young man named Billy who receives a mysterious creature called a Mogwai which he names Gizmo as a Christmas gift. Gizmo comes with three very important rules: never expose it to bright lights, never get it wet and never feed it after midnight. When these rules are broken Gizmo multiplies and spawns into several mischievous creatures known as Gremlins and wreak havoc on the small town of Kingston Falls. They cause mayhem and chaos wherever they go.

Gremlins is the perfect blend of comedy and horror with plenty of laughs and scares along the way. Its mix of practical effects, including puppetry and animatronics brought Gremlins to life in a way that still feels relevant today. Decades later, this film remains a beloved classic and often revisited during the holiday season. It is definitely worth checking out for a fun and thrilling movie night. So sit back, respect the rules and enjoy the show.
